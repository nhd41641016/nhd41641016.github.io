                  Revolutions Before the Age of the Internet

                  The Beginning of History

                  Bliss was it in that dawn to be alive,

                  But to be young was very heaven!—Oh! times,

                  In which the meagre, stale, forbidding ways

                  Of custom, law, and statute, took at once

                  The attraction of a country in romance!

                  —William Wordsworth, "The French Revolution as It Appeared to Enthusiasts at Its Commencement"



                  Revolutions have occurred whenever there is injustice rendered by corrupt leaders or a corrupt system indulged in oppressing people. If we analyze some old revolutions, we can see that, not just one event, but a series of events have led to revolutions and subsequent change of political power. The main differences between the present-day revolutions and the older revolutions lie in the speed of communication and the method of organizing. Modern Internet technology has provided communication at lightning speeds and social media have facilitated the organizing of revolutions at grass-root levels. The other major difference we can see is that there is comparatively less violence in most modern revolutions than in older revolutions.
