                  "Those who make peaceful revolution impossible will make violent revolution inevitable."

                  —John F. Kennedy

                  A revolution is drastic change of any kind. Although the term revolution is mostly used to denote a change in socio-political institutions, it can also be used to denote a change in culture, fashion, technology, or science. When people think of political revolutions, bloody images come to mind. Although there is veracity in this imagination, there have been several instances of non-violent revolutions in the past such as the Indian independence movement led by Mahatma Gandhi culminating in India's independence in 1947 and the Velvet Revolution of Czechoslovakia in 1989 leading to the collapse of communism. More recently,the Tunisian revolution of 2010-2011 and the Egyptian revolution of 2011 where technology and social media played an important role were peaceful for the most part during the ouster of the authoritative regimes.
