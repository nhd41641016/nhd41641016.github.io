                  Origin of the Internet

                  The Internet began as a Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA) project in 1969 originally intended to connect several databases across the country. It was named as the ARPANET. Advances were almost completely technical until the end of 1991. With the National Science Foundation's (NSF) allowing for commercial traffic across its network, the Internet underwent a major transition. The 1990s saw a drastic change of Internet as a major source of communication, allowing people to use it for instant messaging, Voice over IP (VoIP) calls, video calls, and world wide web for blogging and accessing information. With the advancement of smartphones, the Internet has become accessible to people in remote places, thus allowing the real globalization of ideas.
