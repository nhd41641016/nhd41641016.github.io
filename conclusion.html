                  Conclusion

                  Internet and social media vehicles such as Facebook and Twitter made revolutionary thoughts penetrate the minds of common people empowering them to raise their voices against oppression, injustice, poverty, and corruption. Across the countries, developed democracies or not, people took to streets for demanding reform from governments. People in dictatorial regimes such as Tunisia and Egypt revolted in overthrowing governments while those in western democracies such as the U.K. and the U.S. protested against corporate corruption in government and disenfranchisement of weaker sections of the society.

                  Although there has been some debate that social media by themselves have not created revolutions, it is unequivocally true that such media have brought people together in a short amount of time to fight for a common cause.

                  Prior to proliferation of Internet and social media, disgruntled and oppressed people felt helpless in bringing about a change. Frequently, masses received distorted information from government-controlled or corporate-controlled broadcasts. However, because of Internet-based media and tools, common people could instantly disseminate independent information to chains of friend circles and communities helping them organize mass protests against wrongdoings. Examples ranging from Arab Spring to Occupy Wall Street movement show how Internet helped bring into spotlight the atrocities committed against peaceful people by law enforcing agencies such as police and military, especially in insular areas devoid of unbiased professional journalists and traditional broadcasting media not controlled by governments or special interest groups. Uncensored Internet-based media with ordinary people playing the roles of journalists and photographers were instrumental in the release of peaceful activists who were arrested during their struggle. A major difference between social media and the traditional media is that information can be republished thousands of times and transmitted worldwide within a blink of an eye without getting noticed and censored.

                  The Internet has united people around the world by making them sympathetic to the struggles of their fellow inhabitants of the Earth. This true sense of "globalization" did not exist prior to the proliferation of social media. There is a ray of hope that the world will be a better place through understanding of one another's problems.
