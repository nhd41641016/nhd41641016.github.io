                  Web 2.0 and Social Media

                  "This is Revolution 2.0. Everyone is contributing to the content."

                  —Wael Ghonim

                  While Web 1.0 was used primarily in disseminating news articles, blogs, commentaries in just one direction from the originator to on-line readers, Web 2.0 allowed readers to participate via their comments, opinions, or criticisms, thereby enriching user experience about a website. A typical participation platform provides users with interface for typing in text as well as choice menu and buttons for carrying out interacting functions related to the website. Interface for typing in text allows users to write free style comments or opinions. Also, adequate data storage facilities are for storing reader's entries. also software, and storage facilities all through their web browser. Freedom provided by Web 2.0 platforms to end users contributes greatly to the content of an article or a blog.
